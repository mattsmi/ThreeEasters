Notes

Easter Dating Method

Today all Western countries — and almost all others — use the Gregorian Calendar as their civil calendar. The Roman Catholic Church uses the Gregorian Calendar for calculating the date of Easter, as do the Finnish Orthodox Church and several Eastern Catholic Churches (e.g. the Syriac Maronite Church of Antioch, the Melkite Greek-Catholic Church).

Most Eastern Orthodox Churches (e.g. the Ecumenical Patriarchate of Constantinople, the Church of Greece, the Greek Orthodox Church of Antioch) calculate the date of Easter according to the Julian Calendar. However, the Greek Orthodox Churches (e.g. Greek, Antiochian, Alexandrian) use the Revised Julian Calendar (or Milanković Calendar), which is similar to the Gregorian Calendar, for their fixed feasts (sanctoral cycle). Dates in the Revised Julian Calendar will be the same as those in the Gregorian Calendar until 2800. For this reason, they are often considered to be the same.

The Russian Orthodox Church and Mount Athos, and one or two others, continue to use the Julian Calendar for all feasts, however.
